The river has taught me to listen.

Wisdom cannot be passed on. Wisdom which a wise man tries to pass on always sounds like foolishness. Knowledge can be communicated, but not wisdom. One can find it, live it, be fortified by it, do wonders through it, but one cannot communicate and teach it.

Within you, there is a stillness and a sanctuary to which you can retreat at any time and be yourself. Like the river that flows and yet is always there, you too can change and still remain.

I have always believed that the world is not only a play of opposing forces but also a great harmony. What we call evil is only a necessary part of the whole, without which the whole would not exist. The game of the world is not played with good and evil alone, but with all the colors of the spectrum.

  • I have been and still am a seeker, but I have ceased to question stars and books; I have begun to listen to the teaching my blood whispers to me.
  • The bird fights its way out of the egg. The egg is the world. Who would be born must first destroy a world.
  • There is no reality except the one contained within us. That is why so many people live such an unreal life — they take images outside them for reality and never allow the world within to assert itself.
  • You are willing to die, you coward, but not to live. The Magic Theatre offers its doors to those who dare to laugh at the chaos within.
  • Every man is more than just himself; he also represents the unique, the very special and always significant and remarkable point at which the world's phenomena intersect, only once in this way and never again.

When someone who is searching sets out on his journey, it often happens that the day he leaves behind grows alien to him. A new space opens, and he is suddenly no longer at home in his old world. And that is how the path begins.

But I am on that path. And I will not turn back.

I wanted only to live in accordance with the promptings that came from my true self. Why was that so very difficult? Because the self is not a fixed thing — it is a becoming, a river, a journey without end. You do not find yourself once and for all; you build yourself, day by day, with every choice.

The River

He looked into the water and saw that the river was made of nothing but voices — the voices of all who had ever lived, who had ever loved and suffered and died. And all of them were part of the great song of life, the unity that encompasses everything. Nothing was past; nothing was lost. Everything was present, flowing together in the eternal now.

I learned that time is not real. The river is everywhere at once — at the source and at the mouth, at the waterfall and at the mill. It is present in all places at all times. And for the one who truly sees, there is only the present. The river has no past and no future. It only flows.

To understand the world, one must not cling to words. Words are fragments, echoes. But the river speaks a language beyond words — it speaks the truth of what is, without judgment, without division.

The Magic Theatre

The Steppenwolf, the wild man of the steppes, lives in every one of us. We are not one, but many. We are a chaos of forms, of ages, of animals, of beings both human and divine. The error is to think you are a single self, a fixed soul. In truth, your soul is a crowded theater, and every actor is real.

The Magic Theatre is not for everyone. It is only for the mad ones — the ones who have tasted the poison of life and found it sweet. It is for those who understand that laughter and despair are not opposites but brothers. It is for those willing to lose themselves in order to find themselves.

The Glass Bead Game

The game is a playing with all the contents and values of our culture. It is a game that demands serenity, discipline, and the surrender of the ego. The player learns to see the connections between all things — between a melody and a mathematical formula, between a philosophical proof and a constellation in the sky. Everything is connected. Everything is one.

Serenity — Gelassenheit — is not indifference. It is the state of one who has renounced the compulsion to force meaning onto life, and instead allows meaning to reveal itself. It is the highest achievement of the spirit: to remain calm in the face of chaos, to trust the order beneath all things.

Truth is lived, not taught. The path is walked, not mapped. Every doctrine is a cage; every system is a shadow. But the living truth — the word that was in the beginning — that cannot be captured in any net of words.

I loved in you what no one else had loved — the thing that made you different, strange, difficult. I loved your restlessness and your longing. I loved the seeker in you.

There is, deep within you, a being that knows. Not the mind that calculates and judges, but a quieter voice — the voice of the one who has always been. To hear it is the beginning of all wisdom.
