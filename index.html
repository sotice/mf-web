The bird fights its way out of the egg.

The egg is the world. Who would be born must first destroy a world.

The bird is struggling to be born. The egg is the world. He who would be born must first destroy a world. The bird flies to God. The God's name is Abraxas.

I wanted only to try to live in accord with the promptings which came from my true self. Why was that so very difficult?

  • Each man had only one genuine vocation—to find the way to himself.
  • His destiny was to reveal himself to himself.
  • No one has ever been entirely himself; yet each one strives to become himself.
  • Obscurely, each one is a remarkable being, a unique experiment of nature.
  • Life passes by like fire, like a veil flapping in the wind.

Nothing in the world is more distasteful to a man than to take the path that leads to himself.

But I am on that path. And I will not turn back.

I had to taste the world's poison, bite the apple, fall out of the childhood garden into the world. That is the journey.

The Two Worlds

My story began in the world of light and order—the world of my parents, of cleanliness, of goodness. But there was another world, a shadow world, dark and forbidden. And I belonged to both.

Anyone who has tasted the forbidden fruit, who has looked over the fence into the other world, can never go back. He carries the mark.

The awakening of the self within us is like the first taste of the fruit—once you have bitten, you cannot un-bite. You can only go forward, or rot.

The Mark of Cain

Those who bear the mark are not criminals, as the common folk believe. They are the awakened ones—the ones who have dared to be different. They walk through the world with a quiet intensity, and most people sense that there is something about them, something strange and dangerous. That mark is not a punishment. It is a distinction.

Abraxas

We are used to thinking of God and the Devil as separate. Good and evil, light and dark—we split them. But the true deity, the god that encompasses both, is Abraxas. He is the unity of all opposites. To worship him is to accept the whole of existence, not just the clean half.

The bird in the egg knows both the darkness and the light. It does not reject one for the other—it grows through both.

Finding Yourself

No one can teach you who you are. You can read a thousand books, listen to a thousand sermons, and still miss it entirely. The only way to find yourself is to walk into the fire, to let it consume everything that is not you, and to see what remains.

What remains—that is you.

Yes, it is a lonely path. To be awake is to be alone. But I will not trade the fire of waking for the comfortable ashes of sleep. Not now. Not ever.

If you hate a person, you hate something in him that is part of yourself. What isn't part of ourselves doesn't disturb us.

I lived in my dreams. That is what you have never understood. I lived in dreams that were more real than the streets, the houses, the trees outside your window.
